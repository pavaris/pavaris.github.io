Stop 01 · Days 1–3
Sapporo
3 nights · Hokkaido
Day 1
Land at New Chitose (CTS), transfer to the city (~40 min). Settle in, explore Susukino for dinner — try miso ramen and Hokkaido beer. Light evening walk through Odori Park.
Day 2
Full day trip to Jozankei Onsen (~1 hr). Peak gorge foliage lines the Toyohira River; soak in an outdoor hot spring surrounded by red and gold maples. Back for soup curry dinner.
Day 3
Day trip to Otaru (~40 min by train). Walk the historic canal, browse glass-blowing studios, and have the best sushi of the trip for lunch — the seafood here is extraordinary. Return for a final Hokkaido meal.
Don't Miss
🦀 Fresh crab & uni 🍜 Miso ramen 🍦 Hokkaido soft serve ♨️ Jozankei gorge onsen 🍱 Otaru sushi 🍺 Sapporo Beer Museum
Stop 02 · Days 4–8
Kyoto
5 nights · Kansai
Day 4
Fly Sapporo → Osaka, train to Kyoto. Check in and take a slow evening stroll through Gion district — the lantern-lit machiya townhouses at dusk are unforgettable.
Day 5
Fushimi Inari at dawn (before the crowds), then Nishiki Market for late breakfast snacks. Afternoon at Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) and Ryoan-ji rock garden.
Day 6
Full day in Arashiyama — bamboo grove, Tenryu-ji temple garden (with fall color on the pond), riverside lunch, and the monkey park for views over the city.
Day 7
Day trip to Nara (~45 min). Free-roaming deer, Todai-ji (Great Buddha), and Kasuga Shrine's lantern-lined paths. Back for kaiseki dinner in Kyoto.
Day 8
Day trip to Osaka (~30 min by Shinkansen). Dotonbori street food crawl, Kuromon Market, Osaka Castle grounds. Optional: stay for Osaka nightlife and return late.
Don't Miss
⛩️ Fushimi Inari at dawn 🎍 Arashiyama bamboo 🦌 Nara deer park 🍢 Dotonbori food 🏯 Osaka Castle 🫖 Nishiki Market 🌙 Gion at night
Stop 03 · Days 9–14
Tokyo
6 nights · Kantō
Day 9
Shinkansen from Kyoto (~2h 15min). Arrive, check in, and orient yourself in Asakusa — Senso-ji Temple, Nakamise shopping street, and a walk along the Sumida River.
Day 10
Shibuya crossing at rush hour, then Harajuku (Takeshita Street, Omotesando boutiques) and Meiji Shrine — a peaceful forest escape inside the city.
Day 11
Shinjuku Gyoen (early autumn leaf color), then explore Shinjuku's contrasts: department stores by day, Golden Gai tiny bars and Omoide Yokocho yakitori alley by night.
Day 12
Day trip to Nikko (~2 hrs). Ornate Tosho-gu shrine complex, Kegon waterfall, and mountain foliage. Book tickets in advance — very popular in October.
Day 13
Akihabara for electronics and anime, then the quiet streets of Yanaka (old Tokyo feel). Evening: teamLab Planets in Toyosu — book well in advance, sells out weeks ahead.
Day 14
Flexible final morning — revisit a favorite neighborhood, last ramen, or last-minute shopping in Ginza or Shimokitazawa. Head to HND (30–45 min from city center).
Don't Miss
🏮 Senso-ji Temple 🚦 Shibuya crossing 🌿 Shinjuku Gyoen 🎮 Akihabara ⛰️ Nikko day trip 🎨 teamLab Planets 🍶 Golden Gai bars 🍢 Omoide Yokocho
Essential Tips
Book in Advance

teamLab Planets and Nikko sell out weeks ahead in October. Reserve both before you arrive.

IC Card

Get a Suica or ICOCA card at the airport. Works on almost all trains, buses, and convenience stores across all three cities.

Foliage Timing

Hokkaido peaks Oct 10–20. Kyoto and Tokyo peak mid-to-late November — expect beautiful early color in October without the November crowds.

Packing

Layer up. Sapporo nights can dip to 8°C; Kyoto and Tokyo are warmer at 15–22°C. A light down jacket and comfortable walking shoes are essential.
